Meet RSI, or repetitive strain injuries.

Repetitive strain injuries (RSI) are injuries caused or aggravated by repetitive or sustained
submaximal exertion of the bodyâ€™s soft tissue structures including muscles, tendons, ligaments, and nerves.

How does this happen?

RSI is commonly caused by repeating tasks beyond a person's limits for an extended period of time, often without proper rest.

As the tissues & nerves don't have time to heal, scar tissues may build up; thereby causing a loss of strength, pain while performing simple tasks, and numbing sensations.

RSI is musculoskeletal.

The U.S. Bureau of Labor and Statistics reported that musculoskeletal disorders accounted for 26 percent of all workplace injuries at a cost of $45-60 billion in workers compensation and related costs in 2000.

RSI occurs from the smallest of tasks.

The prevalence of reported discomfort ranges by workers at the computer range from 15% to 70% depending upon the type of computer work demands

RSI drains society.

The average number of workdays lost because of RSI is three times the average number of workdays lost for all other types of work-related injuries, and total direct and indirect costs to society were estimated at over $1 trillion.

There is no cure, but RSI can be managed.

The only solutions for someone that has been diagnosed with RSI are:
Painkillers (temporary relief), physiotherapy, and in certain caises, surgery.
